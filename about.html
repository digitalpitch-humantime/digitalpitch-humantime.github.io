About

Digital Pitch, Human Time is an exploration of authorship in music. Rather than composing notes directly, the project defines mathematical systems that generate pitch material, shifting compositional intent from individual decisions to formal structures.

The algorithms used operate on integers, probability distributions, and recursive rules. These systems generate sequences without musical awareness; they do not encode rhythm, harmony, or expression. Their output is purely numerical.

Human intervention occurs after pitch generation. Rhythm, phrasing, articulation, harmonic context, and large-scale form are shaped through listening and intuition. Time is treated as a human domain, distinct from the algorithmic logic that governs pitch.

By separating pitch from time, the project examines how much musical meaning can arise from structure alone, and how much depends on human interpretation. The resulting works exist between determinism and choice, calculation and perception.
